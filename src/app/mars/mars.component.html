Welcome to Mars!

A place we do not call home.

There's Earth: the blue and green planet, filled with life.

And then there's Mars.

Mars Quick Info
  • Radius: 2,106 miles
  • Distance from Sun: 142 million miles
  • Orbit time: 687 days
  • Liquid water: No
  • Atmosphere: Very thin; no oxygen
  • Magnetosphere: No
  • Moons: 2 moons (Phobos and Deimos)

Fun fact: Mars may have had life sometime in the past!

For starters, Mars is far from the Sun, so all of its water is frozen.

This already kills hope for life on Mars.

But let's say you brought water along with you. What could go wrong?

Turns out, a lot could go wrong.

The atmosphere is thin, and it doesn't even have oxygen, so you would suffocate.

Mars doesn't have a magnetosphere, so radiation can easily reach the surface.

And last, but not least, the temperature can go from boiling hot in the morning to freezing cold at night.

Not a great place to stay

And those are some of the reasons why Mars doesn't have life.

However, because there is frozen water on Mars, it indicates that Mars may have had life in the past.

Those days are long gone. Who knows, maybe Earth will be the next Mars some day.
