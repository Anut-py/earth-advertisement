Welcome to Earth!

The place we call home

Wondering where to go for your intergalactic vacation? You're at the right place.

Earth is the perfect planet to go to! Here, we have liquid water, an atmosphere and magnetosphere, and much more!

The Earth is a rocky planet, which means it has solid ground and a (relatively) small size.

Since the Earth is 93 million miles from the Sun, the temperature is cold enough that water doesn't vaporise, and hot enough that it doesn't freeze.

This means that the water is liquid, and it is perfect for life.

Since the Earth is tilted, as it orbits the sun, the Northern hemisphere gets more light and the Southern hemisphere gets less light, or the other way around.

This leads to seasons. Whichever hemisphere has more light has summer, and the other one has winter.

Sometimes, both hemispheres get roughly the same amount of sunlight. This happens twice in the year: once after summer (we call this autumn), and once after winter (we call this spring).

These four seasons happen every year, and they each take the same amount of time.

On the surface of the Earth, we have a mix of water and land, as you can see in the video.

About 71% of Earth is water and about 29% is land. On land we have landforms like mountains, valleys, canyons, etc.

Landforms are variations in the surface of the Earth. This is why the Earth's surface isn't completely flat.

These landforms are formed over time by erosion and weathering.

On Earth we have 2 types of water sources: freshwater and saltwater.

Freshwater is pure water that is safe to drink. It is found in rivers and lakes, and also in glaciers at the poles.

Saltwater is water saturated with salt. Saltwater is not safe to drink, and you might die if you drink too much of it.

Saltwater is found in oceans and seas.

The Earth has an atmosphere made up of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, 0.9% argon, and 0.1% other gases.

The concentration of oxygen is enough for us to breath, but low enough that it doesn't set everything on fire.

The Earth has just one moon. It orbits the Earth every 28 days. Over these days it goes through a number of "phases".

This means that the moon looks different in each phase. This is because only half of the Moon is lit up by the Sun, and at it orbits the Earth, the lit part looks bigger or smaller, depending on the time of the month.

As the Moon orbits the Earth, it causes high tides and low tides. High tides are when the sea level rises, and low tides are when it goes down.

The Moon's gravity pulls the water and causes high tides, and when it releases the water it causes low tides.

The Earth has 4 layers: the crust, the mantle, the outer core, and the inner core.

The crust is the outermost layer, and it is only about 40 miles deep. This is the layer that has all of our continents and oceans.

The crust is in parts, called tectonic plates. These tectonic plates float on the mantle and occasionally crash into each other, forming landforms like mountains.

The mantle is the second layer of the Earth. It is made of molten, semi-solid rock.

The rock in the mantle can flow, but it can only flow very slowly.

The outer core is made of liquid metal. It is made of mostly iron and nickel.

The outer core is hotter than the mantle because the deeper you go into the Earth, the hotter it gets.

Surprisingly, the inner core isn't liquid! It's solid metal, the same metals found in the outer core.

The inner core is very deep in the Earth, and the pressure there is extremely high. This pressure keeps the metal solid, despite the high temperatures.

The spinning metal in the inner and outer core makes a magnetic field around the Earth, which blocks harmful radiation from the Sun.

This magnetic field is called the magnetosphere. Because of the amount of radiation coming from the Sun, it becomes teardrop-shaped, with the pointy part pointing away from the Sun.
